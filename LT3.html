Adding and Subtracting Fractions

Fractions represent division. To add or subtract, denominators must match. Use the LCM of denominators to rewrite fractions with a common base, then add/subtract numerators. Example: 1/2 + 1/3 → 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6.

Multiplying and Dividing Fractions

Multiply numerators together and denominators together. Example: 4/5 × 3/2 = 12/10 = 6/5. For division, flip the second fraction and multiply: 5/6 ÷ 2/3 → 5/6 × 3/2 = 15/12 = 5/4.

Simplifying Fractions

Reduce fractions by dividing numerator and denominator by their GCF. Example: 12/18 → divide both by 6 → 2/3.

Mixed Numbers & Improper Fractions

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions by multiplying the whole number by the denominator and adding the numerator. Example: 2 1/3 → (2×3+1)/3 = 7/3. Reverse the process to go back.

Powers of Fractions

Raise both numerator and denominator to the given power. Example: (2/3)2 = 4/9.

Solving for Variables with Fractions

Isolate the variable by performing inverse operations. Example: (x/4) = 3 → multiply both sides by 4 → x = 12.

Comparing Fractions

Find a common denominator or cross-multiply to compare. Example: 3/4 vs 2/3 → 3×3=9, 2×4=8 → 3/4 is larger.

Equations with Rational Numbers

Apply fraction rules to solve equations. Example: (x/2) + 3 = 5 → subtract 3 → x/2 = 2 → multiply by 2 → x = 4.
